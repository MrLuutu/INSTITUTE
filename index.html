Building Evidence Infrastructure for Africa.

❦
The Institutional Thesis

Africa does not lack health challenges. It lacks durable evidence infrastructure capable of generating, translating, and sustaining high-quality research over decades. LIPH was established to help build that infrastructure.

Explore Research Centers Collaborate With Us
Mission

To develop the evidence, research systems, partnerships, and scientific infrastructure required to improve public health outcomes across Africa.

Vision

To become a leading African research institution advancing childhood health, neuroscience, and population health through evidence generation, research excellence, and institutional capacity building.

Research Centers

Three centers.
One mission.

The Institute is organized around three permanent research centers — each designed for long-term scientific activity, not individual projects. Open each center to explore its scope, research areas, and current priorities.

Generating longitudinal evidence on childhood respiratory, neurological, and developmental health across East Africa.

This is the Institute's flagship scientific center. It anchors LIPH's identity in childhood health — from air quality and lung development to neurodevelopmental outcomes and mental health. Research here is designed for cohort depth.

Building neurosurgical capacity, simulation-based training infrastructure, and health systems evidence for East Africa.

Africa's neurosurgical capacity deficit is among the most consequential health systems failures on the continent. This center produces the evidence and builds the training infrastructure to address it — through rigorous capacity mapping and simulation-based education.
