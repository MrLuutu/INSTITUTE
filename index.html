01 — Institutional Thesis

The gap is not the disease.
The gap is the institutional capacity to study it.

001
We build evidence infrastructure, not just studies.
Single studies answer single questions. Institutional infrastructure answers them continuously. The Lanforte Institute is designed to function as a permanent evidence-generating system — producing research, training researchers, building partnerships, and translating findings into policy across decades, not grant cycles.
002
We start where the evidence gap is sharpest.
Uganda's asthma burden is real and growing. Its correlation with bipolar disorder in paediatric populations — children and adolescents from birth to 18 — is entirely unstudied in Sub-Saharan Africa. That intersection is our initial research mandate. Specific enough to be fundable, significant enough to matter at scale.
003
We build to international standards from day one.
MakSPH-REC ethics alignment, UNCST clearance, SPIRIT and STROBE reporting standards, Fogarty-compatible protocol architecture, ISBD-networked psychiatric research — not aspirational targets, but the operating floor. Research that cannot withstand international peer review is not research we produce.
004
Machine learning infrastructure is a long-term strategic investment.
Applied intelligence in African public health is the logical endpoint of high-quality longitudinal data collection. The Institute builds toward computational epidemiology, GIS-linked health analytics, and predictive disease modelling from the foundation up — governed by ethical AI frameworks designed for African data systems and data sovereignty.

03 — Applied Intelligence

Research Computing & ML Infrastructure

Not artificial intelligence as spectacle. Computational infrastructure as institutional discipline — built carefully, governed rigorously, deployed in the service of African public health systems.

Long-Term Infrastructure Investment
01
Epidemiological Modelling
Computational disease burden modelling for Uganda and East Africa, built on the Institute's own longitudinal datasets. Designed to produce estimates meeting WHO and GBD methodological standards.
02
GIS-Linked Health Analytics
Geospatial analysis of disease distribution, environmental exposure mapping, and health facility access modelling across Kampala and peri-urban Uganda. Linked to air quality and climate data systems.
03
Predictive Disease Surveillance
Machine learning models for early warning of respiratory disease exacerbation and psychiatric episode risk — trained on African paediatric population data with documented bias auditing.
04
Research Data Pipelines
End-to-end data management: REDCap collection, structured cleaning, encrypted storage, open access repositories, and version-controlled analysis environments aligned to FAIR data principles.
05
Ethical AI Governance Framework
All ML systems developed under a documented ethical AI governance framework addressing data provenance, model fairness, African data sovereignty, algorithmic transparency, and community consent.

05 — Vision

African Evidence Infrastructure

Africa does not have a disease problem. It has an evidence infrastructure problem.

The data to understand, predict, and respond to African disease burdens is not being collected. The institutions to collect it systematically do not yet exist at scale. The Lanforte Institute is one attempt to change that — beginning in Uganda, designed to scale across East Africa.

This is not a research project. It is institutional architecture. A permanent evidence-generating system, designed to outlast any single grant cycle, any single researcher, and any single disease focus.

East African Datasets
Longitudinal, IRB-approved, open-access datasets on respiratory, psychiatric, and environmental health outcomes — built to FAIR data principles for international research use.
Policy Translation Architecture
Dedicated MOH Uganda engagement, KCCA school health programme channels, and regional policy brief infrastructure ensuring findings translate into decisions, not just publications.
Research Capacity Building
Fellowship programmes, clinical research methods workshops, and structured mentorship for Ugandan early-career researchers — building the next generation of African health scientists from inside the institution.
North-South Partnership Infrastructure
Formal research MOU frameworks, co-investigator agreement templates, and IRB co-application architecture for genuine peer-level partnerships with U.S., UK, and European academic medical centres.
Grant Pipeline Development
Fogarty D43, Wellcome Trust ARL, NIMH R21, Grand Challenges Canada, EDCTP — a sequenced grant calendar building institutional funding capacity from seed awards to major multi-year grants.
